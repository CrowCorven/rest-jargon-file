infinity `Prev `__  I  `Next `__ -------------- **infinity**: n. 1. The largest value that can be represented in a particular type of variable (register, memory location, data type, whatever). 2. minus infinity: The smallest such value, not necessarily or even usually the simple negation of plus infinity. In ``N``-bit twos-complement arithmetic, infinity is ``2N-1 - 1`` but minus infinity is ``- (2N-1)``, not ``-(2N-1 - 1)``.
