overclock `Prev `__  O  `Next `__ -------------- **overclock**: /oh´vr·klok´/, vt. To operate a CPU or other digital logic device at a rate higher than it was designed for, under the assumption that the manufacturer put some `*slop* <../S/slop.html>`__ into the specification to account for manufacturing tolerances. Overclocking something can result in intermittent `*crash* <../C/crash.html>`__\ es, and can even burn things out, since power dissipation is directly proportional to `*clock* <../C/clock.html>`__ frequency. People who make a hobby of this are sometimes called “overclockers”; they are thrilled that they can run their CPU a few percent faster, even though they can only tell the difference by running a `*benchmark* <../B/benchmark.html>`__ program.
