path `Prev `__  P  `Next `__ -------------- **path**: n. 1. A `*bang path* <../B/bang-path.html>`__ or explicitly routed Internet address; a node-by-node specification of a link between two machines. Though these are now obsolete as a form of addressing, they still show up in diagnostics and trace headers occasionally (e.g. in NNTP headers). 2. [Unix] A filename, fully specified relative to the root directory (as opposed to relative to the current directory; the latter is sometimes called a relative path). This is also called a pathname. 3. [Unix and MS-DOS/Windows] The search path, an environment variable specifying the directories in which the `*shell* <../S/shell.html>`__ (COMMAND.COM, under MS-DOS) should look for commands.
