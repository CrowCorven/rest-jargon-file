WYSIWYG `Prev `__  W  `Next <../X.html>`__ -------------- **WYSIWYG**: /wiz´ee·wig/, /wiss´ee·wig/, adj. [Traced to Flip Wilson's “Geraldine” character c.1970] Describes a user interface under which “What You See Is What You Get”, as opposed to one that uses more-or-less obscure commands that do not result in immediate visual feedback. True WYSIWYG in environments supporting multiple fonts or graphics is a rarely-attained ideal; there are variants of this term to express real-world manifestations including WYSIAWYG (What You See Is *Almost* What You Get) and WYSIMOLWYG (What You See Is More or Less What You Get). All these can be mildly derogatory, as they are often used to refer to dumbed-down `*user-friendly* <../U/user-friendly.html>`__ interfaces targeted at non-programmers; a hacker has no fear of obscure commands (compare `*WYSIAYG* `__). On the other hand, `*EMACS* <../E/EMACS.html>`__ was one of the very first WYSIWYG editors, replacing (actually, at first overlaying) the extremely obscure, command-based `*TECO* <../T/TECO.html>`__. See also `*WIMP environment* `__.
