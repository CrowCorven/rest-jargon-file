boink `Prev `__  B  `Next `__ -------------- **boink**: /boynk/ 1. [Usenet: variously ascribed to the TV series *Cheers*, *Moonlighting*, and *Soap*]v. To have sex with; compare `*bounce* `__, sense 2. (This is mainstream slang.) In Commonwealth hackish the variant ‘bonk’ is more common. 2. n. After the original Peter Korn ‘Boinkon’ `*Usenet* <../U/Usenet.html>`__ parties, used for almost any net social gathering, e.g., Miniboink, a small boink held by Nancy Gillett in 1988; Minniboink, a Boinkcon in Minnesota in 1989; Humpdayboinks, Wednesday get-togethers held in the San Francisco Bay Area. Compare `*@-party* <../0/at-party.html>`__. 3.
