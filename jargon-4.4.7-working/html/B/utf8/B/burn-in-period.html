burn-in period `Prev `__  B  `Next `__ -------------- **burn-in period**: n. 1. A factory test designed to catch systems with `*marginal* <../M/marginal.html>`__ components before they get out the door; the theory is that burn-in will protect customers by outwaiting the steepest part of the `*bathtub curve* `__ (see `*infant mortality* <../I/infant-mortality.html>`__). 2. A period of indeterminate length in which a person using a computer is so intensely involved in his project that he forgets basic needs such as food, drink, sleep, etc. Warning: Excessive burn-in can lead to burn-out. See `*hack mode* <../H/hack-mode.html>`__, `*larval stage* <../L/larval-stage.html>`__. Historical note: the origin of “burn-in” (sense 1) is apparently the practice of setting a new-model airplane's brakes on fire, then extinguishing the fire, in order to make them hold better.
